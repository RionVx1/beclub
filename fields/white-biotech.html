WHITE
BIOTECHNOLOGY

Biology meets industry. White biotechnology — also called industrial biotechnology — uses microorganisms and enzymes to manufacture chemicals, materials, fuels, and products in ways that are cleaner and more sustainable than conventional chemistry.

Biotech · Industrial
Overview

WHAT IS IT?

White biotechnology (also called industrial biotechnology) applies biological systems — microorganisms, enzymes, and cell cultures — to industrial production processes. It powers the manufacture of biofuels, biodegradable plastics, detergents, food additives, and specialty chemicals. Compared to traditional petrochemical processes, it typically offers lower energy consumption, reduced waste, and a smaller environmental footprint.

At BE Club, we explore how fermentation science, metabolic engineering, and enzyme technology are reshaping industries from textiles to energy — and what that means for Algeria's industrial and environmental future.

Fermentation Technology

Using microorganisms like bacteria and yeast to convert raw materials into valuable products — from ethanol and lactic acid to antibiotics and vitamins.

Enzyme Engineering

Designing and optimizing biological catalysts to perform industrial reactions with high specificity, efficiency, and minimal side products.

Metabolic Engineering

Reprogramming the metabolic pathways of microorganisms to maximize the production of a target compound — a core tool of industrial biotech.

Bioplastics & Biofuels

Producing plastics and fuels from renewable biological feedstocks instead of fossil fuels — reducing carbon emissions and plastic pollution.
