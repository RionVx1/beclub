GREEN
BIOTECHNOLOGY

Engineering nature for a sustainable future. Green biotechnology applies molecular tools to agriculture and the environment — creating hardier crops, reducing pesticide use, and building solutions to feed a growing world.

Biotech · Agriculture
Overview

WHAT IS IT?

Green biotechnology focuses on agricultural applications — using the tools of genetic engineering, microbiology, and molecular biology to improve plants, animals, and the ecosystems that sustain them. It encompasses the development of genetically modified organisms (GMOs), biopesticides, biofertilizers, drought-resistant crops, and plant-based pharmaceuticals.

At BE Club, we study how these technologies can address food security, climate adaptation, and sustainable agriculture — while critically examining the scientific, ethical, and regulatory dimensions of GMO research in Algeria and globally.

Transgenic Plants

Plants engineered to carry genes from other organisms — conferring traits like pest resistance, drought tolerance, or enhanced nutrition.

Biopesticides

Biological agents derived from natural materials — including bacteria like Bt — that control pests without harmful chemical residues.

Plant Tissue Culture

Growing plant cells or organs in controlled sterile conditions to propagate disease-free plants or study plant biology in the lab.

Microbiome Engineering

Modifying the communities of microorganisms in soil and plant roots to improve nutrient uptake, growth, and disease resistance.
